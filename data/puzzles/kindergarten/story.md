You're about to tell the kids about the MOP system
of using a `TEAM SELECTION EXAM`{.answer},
where candidates are whittled down through a series of increasingly
rigorous tests, and finally the top names go through multi-year testing
in order to determine the top six students to represent the United States.

Then you think about it a bit more,
and decide maybe this isn't quite the message you shouldn't
be giving to a bunch of kindergarteners.
Perhaps when they are older, it'll make more sense to them.

So instead, you mutter something like,
"uh, just keep practicing, you'll all get better with experience".

"Oh," says the small girl.
She looks around at her classmates, and sighs.
"Why can't everyone be as smart as me?", she laments,
before closing her eyes and resuming trying to count.

_Kids will be kids_, you think to yourself,
as you leave the kindergarteners
to enjoy the rest of their recess.
